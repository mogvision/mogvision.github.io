\[\mathcal{B}\:\text{iography}\]


Morteza (Mori) Ghahremani is a Research Fellow at AIVI, University of Eastern Finland, with experience developing computer vision algorithms for automated image processing. He held a Ph.D. degree in Artificial Intelligence (AI) in 2021, focused on understanding and developing robust AI-based algorithms for creating & analyzing 3D models. His background in computer vision, medical imaging, and remote sensing inform his mindful but competitive approach. Mori is fueled by his passion for developing and applying mathematical theory and modeling to achieve automatic visual understanding. Mori's hunger for knowledge and determination to turn mathematical theories and concepts into action has contributed to his most recent works like the theory of orderly disorder (or chaos theory ) for segmentation & classification of point cloud data or golden ratio for feature extraction from natural images.

Mori considers himself a ‘forever student,’ eager to both build on his academic foundations in computer vision & AI and stay in tune with the latest advances in these fields. His research interests include- but not limited to
■ 3D reconstruction from multi-view images   ■ denoising & image enhancement  ■ SLAM
■ feature detection    □ lip Reading using audio-visual information    ■ gesture recognition
■ point cloud segmentation & classification   □ object recognition   □ rigid registration
□ non-rigid registration   ■ satellite imagery
His current project is the co-registration and integration of MRI&microscopy image data. MRI and microscopy image data provide complementary information about brain tissues. Integrating such data presents fundamental information of, e.g., structure and function at the cellular, tissular and global levels, which are crucial for the understanding of the healthy and diseased brain. However, integrating multimodal data is labor-intensive without automatization. In this project, he has proposed novel artificial intelligence (AI) learning-based networks, named 3DBrainAI, to 1) efficiently co-register and integrate large quantities of MRI and microscopy images from brains using GANs, 2) produce a complete brain model from the lowest possible number of microscopy images using distributed brain databases and a transfer learning approach.
