ADL: Adversarial Distortion Learning
for Image Denoising

April 24, 2022

Image denoising aims to recover latent noise-free two (2D)/three-dimensional (3D) image data \(\mathbf{x}\) from its noisy counterpart \(\mathbf{y}=\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{n}\), where \(\mathbf{n}\) denotes noise that is independent with respect to \(\mathbf{x}\). The captured images by digital imaging devices are often degraded from their latent observation due to the noise so denoising is essential and it is a preliminary step in many computer vision, image processing, and medical imaging applications. In recent years, deep learning methods have been widely used for the enhancement of images. Despite the great success of CNNs in images denoising, the contents of denoised images by such techniques still suffer from poor textural information. Although several works show less tendency toward overfitting and being data-driven, their performance is still highly dependent on the training samples. This aspect of the current CNN techniques limits their generalizability to different sorts of images. These drawbacks motivate us to propose a novel deep learning denoiser that well preserves the textural information without changing the contrast of to-be-denoised images. We introduce a novel multiscale learning scheme to further participate high-level features in the output results during training, so improving texture contents. Our network does not need training data whose distribution is close to that of the test data, so improving the generalizability of the proposed network. ADL achieved state-of-the-art results on computer vision and biomedical images, including BSD68 and CBSD68. A part of the results is reported below.
